Police Declare Terrorist Incident After Two Jewish Men Stabbed in London

Authorities ramp up security measures following the attack amid rising concerns over antisemitism.

By Amara Okafor · World · Published 29 April 2026 at 8:08 PM EDT · Updated 9 May 2026 at 5:21 PM EDT

LONDON | In a distressing turn of events, the Metropolitan Police have said they were treating a stabbing incident involving two Jewish men as a terrorist incident. The incident, which took place late Sunday evening in the predominantly Jewish neighborhood of Golders Green, has sent shockwaves through the community and raised urgent concerns about rising antisemitism in the United Kingdom.

According to eyewitness reports, the two victims were approached by a group of individuals who began to shout antisemitic slurs before launching a violent assault. The victims, aged 34 and 41, sustained serious injuries and were rushed to a nearby hospital, where they are currently receiving treatment. Their condition is reported to be stable, but the psychological impact of the attack is expected to be profound.

Police said they were treating the incident as a terrorist incident, and investigations are underway to identify the suspect or suspects. The declaration of a terrorist incident underscores the severity of the attack and the potential threat posed to the Jewish community in London and beyond.

The Jewish community in London has faced a troubling rise in antisemitism over the past few years, with reports of violent incidents and hate speech becoming seriously common. Community leaders have long warned that the rise of far-right extremism and the proliferation of antisemitic conspiracy theories online have contributed to a climate of fear among Jewish residents.

In a statement released shortly after the attack, the Board of Deputies of British Jews condemned the violence, calling it a "horrific reminder of the dangers faced by Jews in our society today." They urged the government to take decisive action to combat antisemitism and to ensure that law enforcement agencies are equipped to respond effectively to hate crimes.

Security measures have been heightened across London, particularly in areas with significant Jewish populations. Police presence has been increased in neighborhoods such as Golders Green and Stamford Hill, where community members have expressed concerns about their safety. Officers are conducting patrols and engaging with local residents to provide reassurance and gather information about the attack.

As investigations continue, community members are coming together to support the victims and each other. Local organizations have organized vigils and community gatherings to promote solidarity and resilience in the face of adversity. These events aim to foster a sense of unity and strength among residents, as they navigate the emotional aftermath of the attack.

The rise of antisemitism in the UK has been a topic of increasing concern for years, with various reports indicating a surge in incidents. According to the Community Security Trust (CST), a charity that monitors antisemitism in the UK, there were over 1,800 reported antisemitic incidents in 2022 alone, marking a record high. This trend has prompted calls for more robust measures to combat hate and protect vulnerable communities.

In light of the recent attack, many are calling for a renewed focus on education and awareness programs aimed at combating antisemitism and promoting tolerance. Community leaders emphasize the importance of fostering dialogue and understanding among different groups to prevent further violence and division.

As the investigation unfolds, the Jewish community in London remains on high alert, grappling with the implications of the attack. The incident serves as a painful reminder of the challenges faced by minority communities in an increasingly polarized society.

In the coming days, community leaders and local officials are expected to hold meetings to discuss strategies for enhancing safety and security for Jewish residents. These discussions will likely focus on collaboration between law enforcement and community organizations to create a comprehensive approach to preventing hate crimes.

As London grapples with the fallout from this serious incident, the hope is that it will serve as a catalyst for meaningful change in addressing antisemitism and promoting a more inclusive society.
