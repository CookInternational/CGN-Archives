Jury Selection Continues for Indianapolis Mother Charged in Death of 5-Year-Old Daughter

The Marion County trial process is moving forward in a child-death case, with jurors being selected before prosecutors present evidence.

By Monica Steele · Local · Published At: 19 June 2026 at 10:38 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 19 June 2026 at 10:38 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Jury selection continued in Marion County for the trial of an Indianapolis mother charged in connection with the death of her 5-year-old daughter, a court proceeding that requires careful attention to what prosecutors allege, what the defense contests and what has not yet been proven.

The case is one of the most sensitive forms of local court coverage: a child death, a parent facing criminal charges and a jury being seated before evidence is presented. At this stage, the central fact is procedural. A trial process is underway, and the defendant is presumed innocent unless prosecutors prove the charges beyond a reasonable doubt.

What is happening in court

WTHR reported that jury selection was continuing in the trial. Jury selection is the process through which attorneys and the court question prospective jurors to determine whether they can decide the case fairly and based only on evidence admitted in court.

It is not evidence of guilt or innocence. It is the gatekeeping stage before opening statements, witness testimony and exhibits. In high-emotion cases, judges often focus closely on whether potential jurors have been exposed to media coverage or have personal experiences that could affect impartiality.

The charge and the allegation

The defendant is charged in connection with the death of her 5-year-old daughter. Prosecutors will have the burden of proving the specific charges filed in court. The defense will have the opportunity to challenge evidence, cross-examine witnesses and present its own arguments consistent with court rules.

CGN News is not publishing unsupported factual claims about the child's death beyond what is contained in court records or established public reporting. In child-death cases, social media claims, neighborhood speculation and emotionally charged commentary should not be treated as evidence.

Why jury selection matters

Jury selection can shape a trial even before testimony begins. Prosecutors and defense lawyers look for jurors who can evaluate medical evidence, witness credibility, police investigation records and expert testimony without letting sympathy or anger replace the legal standard.

That does not mean jurors must be emotionless. It means they must be able to follow instructions. In a case involving a young child, the court's challenge is to seat jurors who understand the seriousness of the allegations while still applying the presumption of innocence.

What comes next

After a jury is seated, the court is expected to move to opening statements. Prosecutors will outline what they believe the evidence will show. Defense counsel may preview the defense theory or reserve certain arguments for later, depending on strategy.

Witness testimony could include investigators, medical professionals, family members or other people with direct knowledge, depending on what the court permits. Any possible sentence would depend on the charges of conviction and the judge's findings at a later sentencing phase, not on the beginning of trial.

Why careful language matters

Local news has a duty to report serious cases without prejudging them. The public has a legitimate interest in court proceedings, child welfare and prosecutorial decisions. The defendant also has a right to a fair trial. Those interests are not in conflict when coverage is factual, attributed and restrained.

CGN News will continue to treat the case as an allegation until a jury reaches a verdict or the court otherwise resolves the charges. Future coverage should be updated only from court proceedings, filings or official statements.

What this means

The case is significant because it involves a young child and a serious criminal charge, but the public should understand that jury selection is procedural and not proof of guilt.

Readers should watch for opening statements, witness testimony and court filings while remembering that charges remain allegations until proven.
