Derecho Leaves Midwest Power Grid and Chicago Travel Networks in Recovery Mode

Outages, airport delays and blocked roads turn a windstorm into a regional infrastructure test.

By CGN News Staff · Local · Published At: 11 June 2026 at 8:26 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 11 June 2026 at 8:26 AM EDT

CHICAGO | A destructive derecho left hundreds of thousands of customers without electricity across the Midwest and disrupted travel through Chicago, where more than a thousand flights were delayed or canceled. The storm’s broad wind corridor knocked down trees and power lines, blocked roads and forced utilities and local governments into a multiday restoration effort.

Associated Press reported about 390,000 Midwest customers without power Thursday, including nearly 226,000 in Illinois and around 150,000 in Cook County, while Michigan also recorded major outages. FOX Weather described a damaging wind swath extending more than 350 miles from Iowa through Illinois, Wisconsin, Michigan and Indiana, with gusts reported as high as 90 mph.
