CGN Politics Brief: Senate Passes $70 Billion Immigration Enforcement Bill After Fund Fight

The 52-47 vote funds ICE and Border Patrol for three years while leaving a disputed $1.776 billion settlement fund intact.

By Natalie Ward · Politics · Published At: 5 June 2026 at 7:13 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 5 June 2026 at 7:13 AM EDT

WASHINGTON | The U.S. Senate passed a roughly $70 billion immigration-enforcement package after an extended amendment fight over funding for Immigration and Customs Enforcement, the Border Patrol and a disputed settlement fund tied to claims of government weaponization.

The measure passed 52-47 after a lengthy vote-a-rama. The bill would provide additional money for immigration enforcement through the end of President Donald Trump’s term, but it must still clear the House before it can be sent to the president.

The most contentious provision was a $1.776 billion fund described by supporters as compensation for people harmed by government weaponization. Critics argued that its terms were too broad and could be used to reward political allies without sufficient independent review.

A Democratic amendment led by Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer to permanently block the fund failed 49-50. Several Republicans supported restrictions, but the coalition was one vote short of changing the text.

The debate also covered detention capacity, staffing, border operations, oversight and the treatment of migrants. Supporters emphasized enforcement and continuity, while opponents raised constitutional, humanitarian and budgetary objections.

Because the bill moved through the budget reconciliation process, Republicans could pass it without the 60 votes normally required to overcome a filibuster. That procedure narrowed the role of the minority but did not eliminate internal Republican disagreement.

The House previously advanced an outline for additional enforcement money, but differences in legislative language can still require another vote or negotiations between the chambers. A fast timetable does not guarantee identical final text.

The political stakes extend beyond immigration. The fund dispute became a test of whether lawmakers would impose limits on a program closely associated with the president while still supporting the larger enforcement package.
