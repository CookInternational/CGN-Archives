FIFA and Netflix Launch World Cup Game as Sports Media Goes Interactive

The mobile-and-TV title arrives with the tournament and gives Netflix subscribers a new way to participate.

By Rick Ellis · Entertainment · Published At: 5 June 2026 at 6:55 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 5 June 2026 at 6:55 AM EDT

LOS ANGELES | FIFA and Netflix are releasing a new World Cup football game on 11 June, tying an interactive entertainment launch directly to the opening of the expanded 48-team tournament.

FIFA World Cup: Launch Edition will be available through Netflix Games and was developed with Delphi Interactive. The service says Netflix members can play without a separate purchase.

The title includes the tournament’s 48 teams, more than 1,200 players and 16 stadiums. A smartphone can function as the controller while the game runs on a television, lowering the hardware barrier for casual players.

Up to four players can participate, positioning the game as a living-room social product rather than only a traditional console simulation.

The release follows FIFA’s split from EA Sports and shows the governing body building a new gaming identity. Netflix, meanwhile, is using a major global event to strengthen a games business that sits alongside its film and television subscriptions.

The product arrives as sports rights, documentaries, live events, fantasy games and social media increasingly overlap. Entertainment companies want fans to remain inside one platform before, during and after a match.

The launch will be judged not only by downloads but by retention, technical performance, updates and whether casual players continue after the tournament.
