CGN Wire: Asian Chip Markets Reprice AI Growth as Taiwan Demand Stays Strong

Broadcom’s decline and regional selling show how concentrated expectations can overwhelm strong long-term demand.

By Vivian Lau · Markets · Published At: 5 June 2026 at 6:46 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 5 June 2026 at 6:46 AM EDT

HONG KONG | Asian technology markets moved lower as investors reassessed the price of artificial-intelligence growth, even while Taiwan’s manufacturers continued to benefit from strong demand for chips and servers.

Broadcom’s disappointing market reaction triggered weakness across semiconductor shares and contributed to sharp declines in parts of Asia.

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. remains central to advanced AI-chip production, while Foxconn has raised its outlook on server demand.

The selloff illustrates the difference between strong industry demand and the valuation investors are willing to pay for future growth.

Asian indexes are especially exposed because chipmakers, memory producers, equipment suppliers and electronics assemblers carry large weights.

Capital flows can also be affected by the dollar, U.S. yields and geopolitical risk, creating volatility that is not directly tied to one company’s operating results.
