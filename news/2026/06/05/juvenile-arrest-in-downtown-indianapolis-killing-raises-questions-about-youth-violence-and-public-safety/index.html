Juvenile Arrest in Downtown Indianapolis Killing Raises Questions About Youth Violence and Public Safety

A 14-year-old is being held in connection with the fatal shooting of recent Indiana University graduate Brett Scrogham.

By Monica Steele · Local · Published At: 5 June 2026 at 7:07 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 5 June 2026 at 7:07 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Indianapolis police arrested a 14-year-old in connection with the fatal shooting of 23-year-old Brett Scrogham in a downtown parking garage, while prosecutors continued to review what charges and court process will follow.

Police found Scrogham shot inside a vehicle in a parking garage in the 100 block of South Capitol Avenue on 28 May. He was taken to a hospital and later died. Local reporting identified him as a recent graduate of Indiana University’s Kelley School of Business.

Investigators said surveillance footage and other evidence helped identify the juvenile, who was taken into custody on 3 June. Because the suspect is 14, the case initially involves the juvenile justice system and additional decisions by the Marion County Prosecutor’s Office.

Police Chief Tanya Terry described the killing as an apparent random act or possible attempted carjacking. Those descriptions remain investigative assessments and must not be treated as findings of guilt or a final account of motive.

The juvenile has not been publicly identified. An arrest is not a conviction, and the state must prove any allegation through the appropriate court process. Officials have not publicly released every detail of the evidence.

The killing renewed concern about downtown safety, youth access to firearms and the support systems available to families. Those broader questions require separate evidence and should not be used to assign responsibility for one case before adjudication.
