CGN Wire: Philippines Braces for El Niño Pressure on Food, Power and Households

Drought risk could affect crops, hydropower and consumer prices across the archipelago.

By Isabel Reyes · Environment · Published At: 5 June 2026 at 6:42 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 5 June 2026 at 6:42 AM EDT

MANILA | The Philippines is preparing for the possibility that a strengthening El Niño will reduce rainfall, strain agriculture and hydropower, and add pressure to household food and electricity costs.

El Niño can bring drier conditions to parts of Southeast Asia, but the timing and regional distribution vary. Seasonal forecasts should guide preparation without being treated as proof of a nationwide drought.

Rice, corn, fisheries and high-value crops are vulnerable to water shortages and heat. Production losses can raise imports and expose consumers to movements in regional prices and currencies.

Hydropower output can fall when reservoir levels decline, increasing reliance on other fuels at a time when international energy costs are already elevated.

Local governments and water agencies must coordinate irrigation, drinking-water supply, fire prevention and public-health communication before shortages become severe.

Poor air quality from regional fires can cross borders, affecting schools, transportation and health even when the largest fires occur outside the Philippines.

Targeted support for farmers and low-income households can reduce hardship, but officials need transparent triggers and verified local data to avoid waste and delay.
