Indianapolis Natural Yards Challenge the Mowed-Lawn Standard

Residents who let native plants grow are trying to create habitat and reduce maintenance while navigating neighborhood expectations and city rules.

By Rick Ellis · Local · Published At: 8 June 2026 at 9:17 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 8 June 2026 at 9:26 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Indianapolis residents who allow native flowers, grasses and shrubs to replace portions of conventional lawn are turning front yards into small wildlife habitats, challenging a visual standard that has long treated short turf as the default sign of care.
