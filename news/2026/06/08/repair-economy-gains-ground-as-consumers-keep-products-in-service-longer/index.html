CGN Business Journal: Repair Economy Gains Ground as Consumers Keep Products in Service Longer

Repair cafés, right-to-repair laws and household cost pressure are turning maintenance into a larger economic and policy question.

NEW YORK | A growing repair economy is challenging the assumption that broken household products should be replaced, as consumers facing high costs turn to volunteer repair cafés, independent technicians and new legal rights intended to make parts, tools and diagnostic information more accessible.
