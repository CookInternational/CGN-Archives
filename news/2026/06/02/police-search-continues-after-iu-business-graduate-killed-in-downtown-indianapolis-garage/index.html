Police Search Continues After IU Business Graduate Killed in Downtown Indianapolis Garage

Authorities say they are making progress after Brett Scrogham, 23, was shot in a downtown parking garage and later died.

By Monica Steele · Local · Published 2 June 2026 at 7:17 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Indianapolis police continue to investigate the killing of 23-year-old Indiana University graduate Brett Scrogham, who was shot in a downtown parking garage and later died from his injuries.

Fox News reported that Scrogham was shot in an Indianapolis parking garage on Thursday, May 28, and died Saturday, May 30. Police told Fox News Digital that they were making meaningful progress in the investigation but had not issued a formal suspect description at the time of that report.

Public-safety reporting should move carefully. CGN is not publishing suspect descriptions, motives or arrest claims unless they come from police or court records. Early homicide investigations can change quickly, and inaccurate suspect information can harm innocent people and compromise the investigation.

The killing is significant for downtown Indianapolis because parking garages, entertainment districts and late-night corridors are central to public confidence. A single high-profile shooting can affect how residents, students, workers and visitors think about safety even before crime data changes.

The human impact should not be lost. Scrogham was a recent business school graduate at the beginning of adult life. Coverage should avoid reducing him to a crime statistic while still keeping attention on verified facts and the investigation.

For city officials, downtown safety remains a recurring test involving lighting, cameras, garage security, police visibility, nightlife management and coordination between private property owners and public agencies.

What remains unclear is whether police have identified a suspect, whether surveillance footage has narrowed the timeline, and whether the shooting followed an argument, robbery or another circumstance. Those points should not be guessed.

Readers with information should follow police instructions and official tip channels rather than social media speculation.

CGN will update when IMPD or court records provide confirmed developments.
