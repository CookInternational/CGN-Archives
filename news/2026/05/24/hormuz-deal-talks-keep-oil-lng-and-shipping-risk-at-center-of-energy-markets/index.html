Hormuz Deal Talks Keep Oil, LNG and Shipping Risk at Center of Energy Markets

Oil fell on deal hopes, but shipping access, insurance and route security remain unresolved.

By James Holloway · Energy · Published 24 May 2026 at 6:58 PM EDT

NEW YORK | Oil prices fell as investors responded to signs of progress in U.S.-Iran talks, but the energy story still turns on the Strait of Hormuz and the practical timeline for reopening normal flows.

Reuters reported that oil slipped to a two-week low as Washington and Tehran appeared closer to a peace agreement. The report also noted that major disagreements remained and that energy infrastructure and shipping conditions may take time to normalize.

The Hormuz route carried a major share of global oil and LNG shipments before the conflict. Any phased reopening would affect tanker traffic, insurance costs, refining margins and import costs for Asian and European buyers.

Energy markets are therefore reading both diplomatic language and physical-route risk at the same time.

Additional Reporting By: Reuters oil markets; Reuters Iran diplomacy; Reuters Gulf markets

What this means

The practical energy indicators are tanker movement, insurance pricing, Brent and WTI moves, refinery input costs and any formal maritime-security terms released by negotiators.
