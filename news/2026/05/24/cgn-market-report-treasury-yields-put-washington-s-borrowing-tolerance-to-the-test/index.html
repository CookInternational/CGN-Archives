CGN Market Report: Treasury Yields Put Washington’s Borrowing Tolerance to the Test

Rising yields are feeding into mortgage, credit and business-loan costs as markets assess inflation and war-risk pressures.

By Sophie Keller · Markets · Published 24 May 2026 at 12:20 PM EDT

NEW YORK | Higher Treasury yields are testing Washington’s tolerance for borrowing costs as investors weigh war risk, inflation pressure and the government’s fiscal path.

Reuters reported that the U.S. Treasury rout has pushed borrowing costs into sharper focus for the White House, Congress, households and businesses. Rising yields filter through mortgages, credit cards, business loans and federal debt-service costs.

The market pressure comes as policymakers balance hopes for a U.S.-Iran deal against inflation concerns tied to energy, shipping and fiscal spending. Federal Reserve officials have been cautious about rate cuts while inflation expectations remain elevated.

For investors and borrowers, the key issue is whether higher yields are a temporary reaction to war and inflation risk or a longer repricing of U.S. fiscal credibility.

Additional Reporting By: Reuters; Reuters Middle East markets; Reuters Iran diplomacy

What this means

Mortgage rates, business-loan costs and Treasury auctions will give the clearest near-term signal of whether the bond-market strain is easing or spreading through the economy.
