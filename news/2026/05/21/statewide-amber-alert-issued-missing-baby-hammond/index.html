Statewide AMBER Alert Issued for Missing Baby From Hammond

Indiana authorities issued a statewide AMBER Alert for a missing baby from Hammond.

By Natalie Ward · Local · Published 21 May 2026 at 8:20 AM EDT

HAMMOND | Indiana authorities issued a statewide AMBER Alert for a missing baby from Hammond, asking residents to watch for official updates and report information through law-enforcement channels.

FOX59 reported the alert Thursday morning. The Indiana State Police AMBER Alert system is the state’s official public-safety channel for active child-abduction alerts and related updates.

Because AMBER Alerts can change quickly, readers should rely on the latest official alert page, local law enforcement and trusted local news outlets before sharing names, vehicle details or unverified social media posts.

Public cooperation can be important in fast-moving missing-child cases. Anyone who believes they have relevant information should contact law enforcement or follow the reporting instructions included in the active alert.

CGN News is not publishing unsupported personal details beyond the public alert framing. The priority is accurate public-safety information and avoiding confusion while authorities continue the search.

Additional Reporting By: FOX59; Indiana State Police; Indiana AMBER Alert

What this means

Residents should check the latest official AMBER Alert information before sharing details. If you see something that may be connected to the alert, contact law enforcement rather than posting unverified claims online.
