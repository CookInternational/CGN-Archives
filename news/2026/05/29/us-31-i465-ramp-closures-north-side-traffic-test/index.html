U.S. 31 and I-465 Ramp Closures Bring New North-Side Traffic Test

Level Up 31 work is bringing new ramp closures and traffic shifts around U.S. 31 and I-465 on Indianapolis’ north side.

By Natalie Ward · Local · Published 29 May 2026 at 6:56 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | North-side drivers are facing another construction test as the Level Up 31 project moves into a new phase around U.S. 31 and I-465.

WSBT reported on ramp closures affecting Indianapolis drivers, while the official Level Up 31 project page lists temporary traffic shifts and closures involving I-465 ramps to northbound U.S. 31/Meridian Street and ramp access near 106th Street. WTHR also reported on what drivers should know about the closures and traffic changes.

The project is designed to improve capacity, safety and traffic flow in one of the busiest corridors in the region. The short-term cost is confusion. Drivers who use U.S. 31, Meridian Street, 106th Street or I-465 ramps by habit may find familiar movements changed or blocked.

That makes this a reader-service story. Drivers should check routes before leaving, expect longer commute times and avoid relying only on memory through the construction zone. Navigation apps may help, but posted signs and official project updates should guide last-minute decisions.

The north side already carries heavy commuter, school, medical and retail traffic. When ramp access changes, the effect can spread quickly into local roads, roundabouts and neighborhood cut-through routes.

The safest approach is to slow down, leave extra time and treat the work zone as a changing environment rather than a fixed detour.

Additional Reporting By: WSBT; WTHR; Level Up 31

What this means

The practical takeaway is to plan ahead. Ramp closures and traffic shifts around U.S. 31 and I-465 can change daily routines quickly, especially for north-side commuters.
