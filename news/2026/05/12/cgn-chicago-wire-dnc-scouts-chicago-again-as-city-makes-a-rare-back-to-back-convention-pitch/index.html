CGN Chicago Wire: DNC Scouts Chicago Again as City Makes a Rare Back-to-Back Convention Pitch

Chicago is making a renewed pitch for the 2028 Democratic National Convention after hosting in 2024.

By CGN News Staff · Politics · Published 12 May 2026 at 5:02 PM EDT

CHICAGO | Democratic officials are scouting Chicago as a possible 2028 convention host, giving the city a chance at a rare back-to-back national political showcase.

Axios Chicago reported that DNC officials and technical staff were in Chicago this week reviewing the city’s possible 2028 convention bid.

Chicago hosted the 2024 Democratic National Convention and is competing with cities including Philadelphia, Atlanta, Denver and Boston.

The city’s operational argument rests on hotels, convention experience, security planning and the financial success of its 2024 host committee.

The political challenge is whether national Democrats want to return to the same city so soon.

The stakes include tourism revenue, labor work, business activity, protest planning and Chicago’s national image.
