Fed Transition Meets Record-Low Sentiment as Inflation Expectations Rise

Kevin Warsh starts at the Federal Reserve as households report worsening affordability pressure.

By Sophie Keller · Markets · Published At: 23 May 2026 at 5:00 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 23 May 2026 at 5:00 PM EDT

NEW YORK | Reuters reported that Kevin Warsh is taking over the Federal Reserve with inflation climbing, consumer sentiment sliding and oil prices still tied to Middle East risk.

Reuters also reported that U.S. consumer sentiment fell to a record low in May, with inflation expectations rising as gasoline and living costs weighed on households.

Additional Reporting By: Reuters Warsh; Reuters Consumer Sentiment

What this means

The next indicators are bond yields, inflation expectations and Fed language. Markets are watching whether oil-driven inflation changes the policy path.
