Indy 500 Weekend Carries Racing Celebration and Kyle Busch Tributes Into Memorial Day

Carb Day, Caitlin Clark’s grand marshal role and tributes to Kyle Busch give Indianapolis a layered race-weekend story.

By Rick Ellis · Local · Published 23 May 2026 at 9:11 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Indianapolis moved into its biggest racing weekend with Carb Day practice, final Indianapolis 500 preparations and tributes to NASCAR champion Kyle Busch after his sudden death at 41.

Reuters reported that Alexander Rossi is expected to compete Sunday despite a crash earlier in the week that required minor surgeries. Rossi completed Carb Day laps in a backup car, one of several late-week storylines before the green flag.

Busch’s death added a somber note across motorsports. AP reported that he had experienced severe symptoms before becoming unresponsive in a racing simulator, and that no official cause of death had been released. CGN News is not stating a cause of death.

Indianapolis Motor Speedway paid tribute to Busch during race-week activities, reflecting his long connection to the city and the track through Brickyard success, NASCAR history and the broader racing community.

The local weekend also carries basketball crossover energy because Caitlin Clark is scheduled to serve as grand marshal for the Indianapolis 500, linking the Fever’s national spotlight with one of Indiana’s signature sports traditions.

For Indianapolis, the weekend is both civic spectacle and logistical challenge. Traffic, weather, travel, crowd safety and sports emotion all converge around the speedway.

Additional Reporting By: Reuters; Associated Press

What this means

The weekend is bigger than one race. It is a civic event shaped by motorsports history, local traffic, weather planning, national attention and the emotional weight of losing a major NASCAR figure.
