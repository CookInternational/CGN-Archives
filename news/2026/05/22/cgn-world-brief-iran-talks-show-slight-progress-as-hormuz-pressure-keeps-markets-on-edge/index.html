CGN World Brief: Iran Talks Show Slight Progress as Hormuz Pressure Keeps Markets on Edge

U.S.-Iran talks have shown limited movement, but uncertainty over a deal and Strait of Hormuz risk continues to shape energy and global security concerns.

By CGN News Staff · World · Published 22 May 2026 at 8:40 AM EDT

LONDON | U.S.-Iran diplomacy showed signs of limited movement Friday, but the broader global story remains unresolved: whether talks can reduce the risk around the Strait of Hormuz and prevent renewed fighting.

The Associated Press reported that U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio said there had been slight progress in talks with Iran, while uncertainty remained over whether a deal would be reached or war would resume.

Reuters separately reported that investors continued to doubt the likelihood of a clean breakthrough, with oil prices rising as energy markets watched the diplomatic track. The Strait of Hormuz remains central because of its importance to global oil and liquefied natural gas shipments.

The diplomatic issue is not only nuclear policy. It touches shipping lanes, Gulf state security, inflation risk, European energy exposure, U.S. military posture and the limits of pressure diplomacy. UAE adviser Anwar Gargash told Reuters he saw roughly even odds of a deal and warned against renewed fighting.

For global readers, the key question is whether negotiators can move from ceasefire management and tactical proposals toward an arrangement that gives markets and governments enough confidence to treat Hormuz as stable again.

Additional Reporting By: Associated Press; Reuters; Reuters

What this means

For readers, the Iran talks matter because they affect fuel prices, shipping routes, inflation pressure and U.S. foreign policy. Slight progress is not the same as a deal, so markets and governments are still pricing in uncertainty.
