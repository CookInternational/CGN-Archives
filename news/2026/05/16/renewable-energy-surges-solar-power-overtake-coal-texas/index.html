Renewable Energy Surges as Solar Power Set to Overtake Coal in Texas

EIA forecasts a significant shift in Texas energy landscape by 2026.

By Helena Price · Energy · Published At: 16 May 2026 at 4:33 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 16 May 2026 at 4:33 PM EDT

The U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) has announced that solar energy is expected to surpass coal in electricity generation in Texas by 2026. This forecast reflects a broader trend towards renewable energy sources as the state continues to expand its solar capacity.

According to the EIA, Texas has been leading the nation in solar energy growth, driven by declining costs and supportive policies. The state's abundant sunlight and vast land area make it an ideal location for solar farms, contributing to the increasing share of renewable energy in its power grid.

This transition is significant not only for Texas but also for the national energy landscape, as it highlights the growing viability of renewable energy sources in meeting electricity demands. The shift away from coal, which has been a dominant energy source for decades, underscores the changing dynamics of the energy market.

As solar power continues to gain traction, it is expected to play a crucial role in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and combating climate change. The EIA's projections indicate that by 2026, solar energy could account for a substantial portion of Texas's electricity generation, further solidifying the state's position as a leader in renewable energy.

What This Means: The anticipated rise of solar energy in Texas signifies a major milestone in the transition to renewable energy. As solar power becomes more prevalent, it could lead to lower energy costs for consumers and a reduction in reliance on fossil fuels, contributing to a more sustainable energy future.
