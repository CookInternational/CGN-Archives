CGN Politics Brief: Texas Senate Runoff Tests Trump’s Grip on the GOP

A bitter Republican runoff in Texas is drawing national attention because it could affect Senate control and campaign spending.

By Michael Trent · Politics · Published At: 26 May 2026 at 3:30 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 26 May 2026 at 3:30 PM EDT

WASHINGTON | Texas voters are deciding a Senate primary runoff that has become a national test of Republican power, former President Donald Trump's influence and the party's appetite for risk in a seat it has long expected to hold.

Sen. John Cornyn is trying to survive a challenge from Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton, who received Trump's endorsement after Cornyn failed to win the March primary outright. The race has drawn heavy spending and sharp attacks, with Republicans divided over whether loyalty to Trump or general-election electability should matter more.

Associated Press and Reuters reporting framed the runoff as more than a Texas fight. Democrats need to flip seats to change Senate control, and a competitive Texas contest could force Republicans to spend money in a state they would rather treat as safe. The Democratic nominee is state Rep. James Talarico.

The public-policy stakes are also practical. Senate races shape judicial confirmations, federal spending, regulatory oversight, foreign-policy authority and the balance of power in Washington. Texas is large enough that even a defensive race can change national campaign maps.

Additional Reporting By: Associated Press; Reuters

What this means

For readers outside Texas, the runoff matters because Senate control is national. A race that becomes expensive in Texas can pull money, attention and party leadership away from other battlegrounds.

The next watch point is whether Trump's endorsement is enough to move runoff voters, and whether the winner leaves Republicans unified or damaged heading into November.
