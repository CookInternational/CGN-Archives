Russia Keeps Pressure on Ukraine as Kyiv Braces for Possible New Barrage

Russia launched another wave of drones and missiles as Kyiv faced warnings of possible larger strikes.

By Helena Price · World · Published 26 May 2026 at 3:30 PM EDT

LONDON | Russia kept military pressure on Ukraine overnight while Kyiv faced warnings that a larger barrage could follow, deepening concerns about air-defense capacity and diplomatic risk in the capital.

The Associated Press reported that Russia launched more than 100 drones and two ballistic missiles at Ukraine overnight. The attacks came as Kyiv was warned to brace for possible additional strikes, while foreign missions weighed the warning and several Western diplomatic offices indicated they would remain in the capital.

The pressure follows a broader pattern of Russian strikes aimed at exhausting Ukraine's air defenses, damaging infrastructure and forcing political decisions under threat. Ukraine has continued to call for more air-defense support, especially as U.S. attention and resources are also pulled toward the Middle East.

The war's next stage may be shaped not only by front-line movement, but also by the ability of cities to withstand repeated aerial attacks. Kyiv's resilience depends on air defense, emergency response, diplomatic presence and public morale.

Additional Reporting By: Associated Press; Reuters

What this means

For readers, the immediate concern is escalation risk in a major European capital and the continued strain on Ukraine's air defenses.

The next items to watch are whether Russia follows through with larger strikes, whether embassies change posture and whether Ukraine receives more missile-defense support.
