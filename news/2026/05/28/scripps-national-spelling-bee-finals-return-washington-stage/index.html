Scripps National Spelling Bee Finals Return to Washington Stage

The Scripps National Spelling Bee finals returned to Washington with young spellers competing at DAR Constitution Hall.

By Rick Ellis · Entertainment · Published At: 28 May 2026 at 7:01 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 28 May 2026 at 7:01 PM EDT

WASHINGTON | The Scripps National Spelling Bee returned to a Washington stage this week, bringing hundreds of young spellers to DAR Constitution Hall for one of the country’s most recognizable academic competitions.

Scripps said the 2026 competition features 247 spellers from across the country and around the world. The Bee returned to Washington, D.C., for the first time in 15 years, with competition scheduled May 26-28 and the finals airing live in primetime on ION.

The spelling bee works because it is both simple and brutally difficult. A student stands at a microphone, asks questions about language and then has to turn study, memory, poise and nerve into a sequence of letters. There is no teammate to pass to and no clock to hide behind.

The event also has a deep cultural hold because it turns academics into a national stage. Young spellers become temporary household names, families watch with the tension of a playoff game and viewers at home test themselves against words they may never have heard before.

Scripps said this year’s field includes returning veterans and first-time competitors. The competition’s return to Washington gives the Bee a more symbolic setting, surrounded by monuments, museums and national institutions.

For readers, the Bee is a reminder that education stories can still command attention when they are built around skill, pressure and character. The finals are not just about spelling. They are about preparation, confidence and the courage to stand alone in public with everything on the line.

What this means

The Bee matters because it turns learning into a national event. For students, it is a test of language and courage; for viewers, it is a reminder that academic excellence can still fill a stage.
