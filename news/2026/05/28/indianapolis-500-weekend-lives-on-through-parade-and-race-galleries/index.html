Indianapolis 500 Weekend Lives On Through Parade and Race Galleries

Race-weekend photo coverage keeps Indianapolis’ biggest annual sports-and-culture moment in view.

By Rick Ellis · Entertainment · Published 28 May 2026 at 8:03 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Indianapolis is still living inside the afterglow of race weekend, with parade and race galleries giving residents one more way to revisit the city’s biggest annual sports-and-culture moment.

Indianapolis Monthly published photo coverage of the 500 Festival Parade and the Indianapolis 500, capturing the civic ceremony, fans, drivers and visual energy that define late May in the city.

The value of those galleries is more than nostalgia. Race week helps tell Indianapolis’ story to visitors, neighborhoods, sponsors and families who organize traditions around the month of May.

For local readers, the next question is how the city carries that momentum into June events, summer tourism and downtown activity after the Speedway crowds leave.

Additional Reporting By: Indianapolis Monthly; Indianapolis Monthly

What this means

For readers, the galleries preserve the visual record of race weekend and point toward the city’s next challenge: turning May’s energy into a strong summer calendar.
