Spurs Carry New Momentum Into Game 4 as Knicks Try to Protect Their NBA Finals Lead

San Antonio’s 115-111 victory narrowed New York’s series advantage to 2-1 before another game at Madison Square Garden.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published At: 10 June 2026 at 9:04 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 10 June 2026 at 9:04 AM EDT

NEW YORK | The San Antonio Spurs enter Wednesday’s NBA Finals Game 4 with evidence that the series is more competitive than the New York Knicks’ early lead suggested. San Antonio’s 115-111 victory in Game 3 ended New York’s long postseason winning streak and reduced the Knicks’ series advantage to 2-1.

Game 4 is scheduled for Wednesday evening at Madison Square Garden, with the official schedule listing an 8:30 p.m. Eastern tip. Viewers should check the NBA schedule for any update.

The Spurs changed the series by controlling critical fourth-quarter possessions in Game 3. De’Aaron Fox and Stephon Castle made important plays late.

Victor Wembanyama continued to affect the game as a scorer, rebounder and rim protector. His size changes how New York attacks the paint.

The Knicks received production from OG Anunoby and other starters but were unable to close the final possessions.

Turnovers and assist quality will be important. San Antonio wants the game to become a contest of length, transition and defensive disruption.

New York wants Jalen Brunson to organize half-court possessions without forcing every possession into an isolation.

Karl-Anthony Towns can stretch the floor and create difficult choices for Wembanyama. His screening and passing can be as important as scoring.

Mikal Bridges and Josh Hart give New York defensive versatility. They also need to convert transition opportunities.

The Spurs’ young core has shown composure. Castle’s ability to defend and attack without dominating the ball supports Fox.

San Antonio must avoid excessive fouls. Giving New York free throws can remove the value of good half-court defense.

The Knicks need better control of defensive rebounds. Allowing second chances makes every stop more difficult.

All three games have remained competitive late. The series has not produced a clear tactical mismatch.

New York still holds the advantage because it leads 2-1 and remains at home. San Antonio has changed the emotional direction.

A Spurs win would create a 2-2 series and a best-of-three finish. A Knicks win would create a 3-1 lead.

The official injury report should be reviewed close to tipoff. Morning reports indicated no Knicks players listed and one Spurs listing, but participation status can change.

CGN News will not speculate about unconfirmed injuries or lineups. Coaches may also change starters for tactical reasons.

The atmosphere at Madison Square Garden will be intense after disorder at a separate watch party led to arrests and injured officers. Arena security and city officials will manage crowds.

Most fans participated peacefully. Public safety concerns should not define the entire fan base.

Game 4 will test New York’s ability to respond to its first significant setback of the series. Championship teams must adjust without abandoning their identity.

San Antonio must prove that Game 3 was not only a strong night. The Spurs need the same defensive discipline and late-game execution.

Wembanyama and Brunson are the central stars, but role players may determine the game through shooting, rebounding and turnovers.

The series has become a contest of patience. Both teams have enough talent to score, and the winner may be the team that makes fewer rushed decisions.

No result should be predicted as certain. Game 4 begins with New York leading and San Antonio carrying momentum.
