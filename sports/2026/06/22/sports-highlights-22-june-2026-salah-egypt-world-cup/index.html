Sports Highlights for 22 June 2026: Salah Leads Egypt to Historic World Cup Breakthrough

Egypt’s 3-1 comeback over New Zealand gives Mohamed Salah and the Pharaohs a defining moment in Group G.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published At: 22 June 2026 at 8:36 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 22 June 2026 at 8:36 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Mohamed Salah gave Egypt the kind of World Cup moment its supporters have waited generations to see, helping lead a 3-1 comeback victory over New Zealand in Vancouver that Reuters and The Guardian described as Egypt’s first win at the tournament.

New Zealand struck first, but Egypt took control after halftime. Reuters reported that Salah thrived in a more central role under coach Hossam Hassan, scoring and assisting as Egypt overturned the match. The Guardian’s live coverage also highlighted the second-half pressure that changed the game.

The result matters beyond one scoreline. Egypt has qualified for World Cups before, but this win changes the emotional register of the campaign. Salah is not only the country’s most recognizable player; he is the player opponents plan around. When he influences a match directly, Egypt’s ceiling changes.

The tactical adjustment was central to the story. Playing Salah through the middle allowed him to connect with runners, attack space and become more than a wide threat. Omar Marmoush, Mostafa Ziko and Mahmoud Trezeguet all fit into a second-half surge that turned possession and pressure into goals.

Egypt’s Group G position improved with the win, but the tournament remains open. FIFA match information and Reuters reporting point to a group still shaped by the remaining fixtures. Egypt’s next test will determine whether the breakthrough becomes a pathway to the knockout stage or simply a historic night.

For New Zealand, the defeat hurts because the lead was there. The All Whites have built credibility, but the second-half fade against Egypt showed how quickly World Cup matches punish lapses in control and set-piece defending.

The broader sports lesson is familiar: star power matters most when it changes the team around it. Salah did not merely add a goal. He helped give Egypt structure, belief and a way to turn pressure into history.
