Brendan Fraser Returns to Indianapolis as Indy 500 Honorary Starter

The Academy Award-winning actor, who was born in Indianapolis, will wave the green flag for the 2026 Indianapolis 500.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published At: 16 May 2026 at 12:18 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 16 May 2026 at 12:18 PM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Brendan Fraser is coming back to Indianapolis with one of the most visible jobs in American racing: honorary starter for the Indianapolis 500.

The Academy Award-winning actor will wave the green flag for the 2026 Indianapolis 500 on 24 May from the flag stand at Indianapolis Motor Speedway. The role places Fraser at the ceremonial center of a race that remains one of the defining events in Indiana sports.

Fraser was born in Indianapolis, giving the announcement a hometown connection that fits the Speedway’s tradition of mixing racing history, celebrity appearances, military recognition and Memorial Day weekend pageantry. For many viewers, the honorary starter is a brief but memorable part of the broadcast. For the race, it is another way to connect motorsports to broader culture.

The Indianapolis 500 is not only a race. It is an annual civic event for central Indiana, a national television property, a tourism driver and a ritual that blends speed, engineering, ceremony and local pride. Reserved seats for the 2026 race are sold out, according to Associated Press reporting, underscoring the event’s continued pull.

Fraser’s appearance also arrives during a busy moment in his career. He won the 2023 Academy Award for Best Actor for his performance in “The Whale,” and he has remained a familiar figure to audiences through earlier roles in “The Mummy” franchise and other films. AP reported that he recently portrayed General Dwight D. Eisenhower in “Pressure,” a film connected to the 72 hours before the D-Day invasion, with previews tied to Memorial Day weekend and a wider release afterward.

That timing gives the Speedway a natural Memorial Day weekend bridge. The Indianapolis 500 has long incorporated military tributes, flyovers and remembrance into race-day ceremony. An honorary starter associated with a D-Day film gives organizers a public-facing connection between Hollywood, history and the race’s holiday setting.

For Indianapolis, Fraser’s selection is also a reminder of the city’s cultural reach. Sports events often bring back local figures who left for national careers. When they return in ceremonial roles, the moment becomes more than a celebrity appearance; it becomes a civic callback.

For fans at the track, the green flag is the release point. Months of preparation, practice, qualifying, engineering and promotion collapse into one instant when the starter signals the beginning of 500 miles. The honorary starter does not decide the race, but that person becomes part of the race-day memory.

For IndyCar and the Speedway, celebrity participation can help expand attention beyond core racing audiences. The challenge is always to honor the race’s tradition while welcoming casual viewers who may tune in because of the broader spectacle.

The racing story will still be decided on track: pace, strategy, pit stops, reliability, weather, traffic and the final laps. But Fraser’s role adds a warm local note to the day. An Indianapolis-born actor returning to wave the green flag at the city’s signature global sporting event is exactly the kind of ceremony the 500 knows how to use.

What this means

For Indianapolis readers, Fraser’s honorary starter role adds a hometown connection to one of the city’s biggest annual events.

For the Speedway, the announcement links racing tradition, Hollywood visibility and Memorial Day weekend ceremony.

The race itself will still be decided by drivers and teams, but the green-flag moment gives the 2026 Indy 500 another memorable storyline.
