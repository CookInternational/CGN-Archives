Indy 500 Week Builds With Practice, Anthem Spotlight and a Full May Sports Calendar

Jordin Sparks will return to sing the national anthem as Indianapolis Motor Speedway moves deeper into practice and qualifying week.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published At: 14 May 2026 at 6:19 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 14 May 2026 at 6:19 PM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | The month of May is tightening around the Indianapolis 500, and the race week story now has both track activity and a familiar anthem voice attached to it.

Associated Press reported that Grammy-nominated artist Jordin Sparks will sing the national anthem before the 2026 Indianapolis 500 on May 24. Race organizers said Sparks previously performed the anthem before the 2015 and 2024 races.

Indianapolis Motor Speedway’s event schedule lists Indianapolis 500 practice for May 14-15 and May 18, with Armed Forces Qualifying scheduled for May 16-17. That puts teams and drivers in the critical window where speed, traffic balance, setup confidence and weather all begin to shape the final race narrative.

The anthem announcement matters because the Indy 500 is as much civic ritual as sporting event. The race blends military tributes, music, family traditions, driver introductions, flyovers, and the long buildup to the command to start engines. A returning anthem performer gives the event another familiar touchpoint for fans.

On track, practice week is about information. Teams test how cars behave in traffic, how they respond to changing temperatures and how much qualifying speed can be found without sacrificing race-day comfort. Drivers want confidence, but they also want clean laps and avoidable risk kept to a minimum.

Qualifying weekend will narrow the emotional focus. The fastest cars will chase pole position and front-row prestige. Others will fight to stay out of danger. In Indianapolis, simply making the field can become one of the most intense stories of the month.

The race also sits inside a crowded sports calendar. The Pacers, Fever, college sports, baseball and national events all compete for attention, but the Indianapolis 500 remains the city’s defining annual sports stage. When the Speedway is active, the whole region feels it through hotels, restaurants, traffic, neighborhood gatherings and media attention.

The practical fan checklist is simple: watch the weather, know the schedule, plan parking and arrival times early, and understand that practice speed is not always race speed. A fast Thursday can become a difficult qualifying weekend. A quiet practice can hide a strong race car.

For Indianapolis, this is the week when anticipation becomes visible. The Speedway is open, the field is taking shape, and the traditions around the 500 are now moving from calendar notes into lived city rhythm.

Additional Reporting By: Associated Press; Indianapolis Motor Speedway

What this means

For fans, the next several days decide the mood of race week: practice comfort, qualifying speed, weather and the buildout of race-day traditions.

The next things to watch are qualifying order, weather at the Speedway, team setup trends and whether any driver struggles to find speed before the field is locked.
