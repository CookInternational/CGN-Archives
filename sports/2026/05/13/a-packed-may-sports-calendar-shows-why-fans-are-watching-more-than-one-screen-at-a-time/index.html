A Packed May Sports Calendar Shows Why Fans Are Watching More Than One Screen at a Time

Modern sports viewing is built around overlapping games, practice sessions and national windows.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published 13 May 2026 at 1:58 PM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | The modern sports fan does not watch a calendar anymore. The modern sports fan manages traffic. On a busy May day, baseball, playoff basketball, hockey, racing, tennis, cycling and WNBA coverage can all overlap. The result is not just more sports. It is a different way of watching.

WTOP’s sports listings for the week show how crowded the windows are, with MLB games, NBA Combine coverage, playoff basketball, NHL action, WNBA games, tennis and cycling all sharing attention. The Indianapolis Motor Speedway schedule adds Indy 500 practice to the mix, giving Central Indiana a local anchor inside a national sports crush.

This is why second screens have become normal. Fans check box scores while watching a playoff game, follow practice speeds while listening to baseball and switch to highlights before bed. Leagues are not only competing with one another. They are competing with every notification on the phone.

For broadcasters, the crowded calendar is both opportunity and problem. More live inventory means more reasons for fans to subscribe, but it also means fewer events feel singular. The challenge is to create moments that cut through the clutter.

For fans, the best strategy is choosing anchors. In Indianapolis, the Speedway can be that anchor. Nationally, playoffs and WNBA openings may drive the evening. The rest becomes a personal sports dashboard.

Additional Reporting By: WTOP sports listings; Indianapolis Motor Speedway

What this means

Fans should plan around overlapping windows rather than expecting to watch everything live. Highlights, condensed games and alerts are now part of the routine.

Leagues that create clear storylines will have the best chance to stand out in a crowded May sports environment.
