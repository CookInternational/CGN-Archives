Sports Highlights for 28 May 2026: Knicks Finals Run, NBA AI Officiating and Indy 500 Aftermath Lead the Board

The Knicks’ Finals run, AI-assisted officiating and Indy 500 aftermath lead the morning sports board.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published At: 28 May 2026 at 8:04 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 28 May 2026 at 8:04 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | The sports board starts with the New York Knicks’ return to the NBA Finals, a new push toward AI-assisted officiating and the lingering civic glow from another Indianapolis 500 weekend.

Reuters reported that the Knicks swept the Cavaliers and reached the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999. That gives New York one of the biggest sports storylines of the summer and adds another layer to an already crowded championship calendar.

Reuters also reported that NBA Commissioner Adam Silver said the league will move toward AI-supported systems for objective calls such as out-of-bounds decisions. Officials would still handle judgment calls involving contact and fouls, but possession calls could become faster and more automated.

In Indianapolis, the Indy 500 aftermath remains part of the local sports and culture calendar. Race-weekend coverage, parade galleries and civic events continue to shape the city’s post-Memorial Day mood.

The common thread is scale. Pro sports are not only games. They are transportation events, technology tests, media products and civic identity all at once.
