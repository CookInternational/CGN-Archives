IMS Scoring Pylon Remains One of the Indy 500’s Most Recognizable Race-Day Landmarks

The Indianapolis Motor Speedway scoring pylon remains a race-day reference point and a familiar part of Indy 500 history.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published 21 May 2026 at 8:17 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | The Indianapolis Motor Speedway scoring pylon remains one of the Indy 500’s most recognizable race-day landmarks, carrying the running order, lap count and visual memory of generations of race fans.

ValleyCentral reviewed the pylon’s history as race weekend approaches, while Indianapolis Motor Speedway materials highlight how the modern pylon has been updated to keep the feature visible and useful for fans at the track.

The pylon’s history reaches back to the late 1950s, when the original scoring tower became part of the Speedway’s front-stretch identity. Later versions changed with technology, but the basic purpose stayed familiar: give the crowd a central reference point during a fast-moving race.

The modern scoring pylon is also a reminder that the Indy 500 is both a sporting event and a civic ritual. Fans recognize the cars, the pagoda, the yard of bricks and the pylon as part of the same visual language of May in Indianapolis.

As the field prepares for Sunday, the pylon will again serve a practical role for spectators trying to follow strategy, position changes and late-race drama across 200 laps.

Additional Reporting By: ValleyCentral; Indianapolis Motor Speedway; Indianapolis Motor Speedway

What this means

The pylon gives fans a simple way to follow a complicated race. For people watching from the grandstands or on television, it remains part of the shared Indy 500 experience.
