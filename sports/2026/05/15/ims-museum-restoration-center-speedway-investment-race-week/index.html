IMS Museum Restoration Center Adds Another Speedway-Area Investment Before Race Week

The Indianapolis Motor Speedway Museum has broken ground on a restoration and event center tied to the track’s year-round identity.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published At: 15 May 2026 at 7:26 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 15 May 2026 at 7:26 PM EDT

SPEEDWAY | The Indianapolis Motor Speedway Museum is moving forward with a restoration and event center project that adds another layer to Speedway’s year-round identity beyond the month of May.

WRTV reported that the museum broke ground on the new Restoration and Event Center. IBJ has reported the project is tied to a broader facility plan that includes restoration bays and event space overlooking the workshop and storage areas.

The project matters because IMS is more than a racetrack. It is a cultural institution, tourism engine, motorsports archive and economic anchor for Speedway and Indianapolis. The museum’s ability to restore, display and interpret racing history helps extend the value of the track beyond race day.

Restoration work is also part of preserving motorsports history. Race cars are not static artifacts in the same way paintings or documents can be. They are mechanical objects that require specialized knowledge, parts, documentation and careful maintenance.

Event space adds another business function. It can help the museum generate revenue, host private and public events, and bring more people to Speedway outside the Indianapolis 500 and Brickyard weekends.

The timing is natural. Race week draws national and global attention to the track, and new construction signals that Speedway’s institutions are still investing in the future even while celebrating the past.

The key question is whether the project increases public access to restoration work or mainly supports behind-the-scenes operations. Either way, it strengthens the museum’s role in preserving the story of American motorsports.
