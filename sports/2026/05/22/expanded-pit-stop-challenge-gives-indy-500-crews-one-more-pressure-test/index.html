Expanded Pit Stop Challenge Gives Indy 500 Crews One More Pressure Test

The Pit Stop Competition puts crews in the spotlight before race day, where pit execution can decide track position and momentum.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published At: 22 May 2026 at 5:36 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 22 May 2026 at 5:36 PM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | The Indy 500 spotlight turned from drivers to crews Friday as the Pit Stop Competition gave teams one more pressure test before race-day execution becomes part of the result.

Pit stops can decide track position, strategy flexibility and late-race momentum at Indianapolis Motor Speedway. A clean stop can rescue a difficult stint; a slow or messy stop can erase speed that took hours to build.

The expanded Carb Day format also gives fans a closer look at the people who work behind the wall. Tire changers, fuelers, crew chiefs and spotters are rarely the public face of the race, but the 500 is often won by the full operation rather than one fast car alone.

Sunday’s race will still depend on weather, traffic, caution timing and strategy, but Friday’s pit-lane event is a reminder that execution under pressure remains one of the race’s oldest truths.

Additional Reporting By: INDYCAR; Indianapolis Motor Speedway

What this means

For race fans, the Pit Stop Competition is more than entertainment. It previews which teams look sharp under timed pressure and reminds viewers to watch pit road closely when Sunday strategy begins to unfold.
