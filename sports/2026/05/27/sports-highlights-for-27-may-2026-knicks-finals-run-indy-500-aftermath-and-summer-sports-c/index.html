Sports Highlights for 27 May 2026: Knicks Finals Run, Indy 500 Aftermath and Summer Sports Calendar Take Shape

The Knicks are headed to the NBA Finals, while Indianapolis continues to process a historic Indy 500 finish.

By Derek Gearhardt · Sports · Published 27 May 2026 at 7:46 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | The sports calendar has moved from race weekend into a packed early-summer stretch, with basketball history and Indianapolis Motor Speedway still driving conversation.

Reuters reported that the New York Knicks swept the Cleveland Cavaliers to reach the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999. The win gives New York a long-awaited championship stage and resets the national basketball conversation around a franchise that has spent decades chasing this moment.

In Indianapolis, Associated Press reported that Felix Rosenqvist won the Indianapolis 500 in the closest finish in race history, edging David Malukas by 0.023 seconds after a late restart. The race remains a major local and national sports story because of the finish, the crowd and the significance of a first-time 500 winner.

Together, the stories show how summer sports can shift quickly: one city celebrates a long basketball drought ending, while Indianapolis reflects on a race that will be replayed for years.
