Midwest Derecho Leaves Hundreds of Thousands Without Power

A widespread straight-line wind event creates continuing safety risks after the thunderstorms move east.

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published At: 11 June 2026 at 8:16 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 11 June 2026 at 8:16 AM EDT

CHICAGO | A derecho that swept from Iowa through Illinois, Wisconsin, Michigan and Indiana left hundreds of thousands of customers without electricity and produced widespread wind damage. FOX Weather reported a damage corridor exceeding 350 miles and gusts reaching as high as 90 mph in parts of the region, while Associated Press counted about 390,000 customers without power Thursday.

What this means

Do not approach downed lines or assume they are safe because the neighborhood is dark. Report them and wait for trained crews.

Run generators outdoors at least 20 feet from windows, doors and vents, and use a working carbon-monoxide detector.

Keep refrigerator and freezer doors closed and follow official food-safety guidance. When safety is uncertain, discard vulnerable food rather than tasting it.
