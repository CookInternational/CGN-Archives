Daily Weather Brief for 14 June 2026: Damaging Winds, Isolated Tornadoes and Flooding Threaten Central Indiana

Storms, heavy rain and saturated ground create risks for damaging gusts, isolated tornadoes, road ponding and renewed flooding before cooler, less humid conditions arrive Monday.

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published At: 14 June 2026 at 12:41 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 14 June 2026 at 12:41 PM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Central Indiana remains under a storm and flooding threat Sunday, with the National Weather Service identifying damaging wind gusts, isolated tornadoes, heavy rainfall, road ponding and small hail among the principal hazards.

The Indianapolis forecast calls for thunderstorms during the morning followed by scattered showers or storms later, with a high near 78 degrees. A cooler and less humid pattern is expected Monday with partly sunny conditions and a high near 71.

The National Weather Service Indianapolis page listed a Flood Warning, Special Weather Statement and Hazardous Weather Outlook during the update window. Readers should check the official page because warnings and statements can change quickly by location.

Saturated ground from repeated rainfall increases the chance that additional heavy rain will run off rapidly. Damaging gusts can also bring down limbs or trees more easily where soils are waterlogged.

Today’s Main Threats

Damaging wind is the most widespread severe-weather concern, with isolated tornadoes and small hail also possible in stronger storms.

Heavy rainfall can reduce visibility and produce rapid ponding even outside a formally warned area.

Flooding Requires Immediate Caution

Flood warnings apply to specific locations and waterways. Do not assume that a familiar road is safe because it was passable earlier.

Never drive around a barricade or into water covering the roadway. The pavement may be damaged or the current stronger than it appears.

Warnings May Be Issued Quickly

A watch means conditions are favorable; a warning means a hazard is occurring or imminent in the warned area.

Keep Wireless Emergency Alerts enabled and use NOAA Weather Radio or official NWS channels for location-specific information.

Outdoor Events Need Shelter Plans

Lightning can strike well away from the heaviest rain. Organizers should identify substantial shelter and communicate before storms arrive.

Tents, trees and open pavilions are not safe lightning shelters.

Power and Tree Damage Are Possible

Charge devices and avoid downed lines. Report outages and hazards to the appropriate utility or emergency service.

Do not attempt to remove large limbs during active storms.

Monday Offers Improvement

Partly sunny, less humid weather is expected Monday, with temperatures in the lower 70s.

Flooding or cleanup problems can continue after rain ends, so local warnings and road closures remain important.

What Is Confirmed

The Indianapolis area forecast includes thunderstorms and showers Sunday with a high near 78 degrees.

The official NWS office listed flood and hazardous-weather products during the update period.

Damaging winds, isolated tornadoes, heavy rain, ponding and small hail are identified hazards.

Monday is expected to be partly sunny, cooler and less humid with a high near 71.

What Remains Unclear

No forecast can identify the exact neighborhood that will receive the strongest storm far in advance.

Warnings, rainfall totals and storm timing can change through the day.

Localized flooding depends on storm tracks, drainage and prior rainfall.

What to Watch Next

Monitor weather.gov/ind and local emergency alerts throughout the day.

Move indoors when thunder is heard and remain sheltered until the storm has safely passed.

Avoid flooded roads, downed lines and damaged trees.

Continue checking warnings after rain ends because water levels can rise later.
