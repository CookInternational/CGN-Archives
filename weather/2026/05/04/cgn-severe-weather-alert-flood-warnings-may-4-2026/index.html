Severe Weather Alert: Flood Warnings and Advisories in Effect

Multiple Flood Warnings and Lake Wind Advisory Impacting Regions

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published At: 4 May 2026 at 2:25 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 9 May 2026 at 4:59 PM EDT

The National Weather Service (NWS) has issued multiple severe weather alerts on 4 May 2026, including flood warnings and a lake wind advisory affecting various areas.

A Lake Wind Advisory has been issued for several counties in Kentucky, effective from 1:15 PM CDT until 8:00 PM CDT. Winds are expected to be strong, which may create hazardous driving conditions, particularly for high-profile vehicles. Residents are advised to exercise caution while driving.

In addition, several Flood Warnings have been issued across Indiana and Illinois:

Flood Warning for Edwards, IL; Gallatin, IL; Wabash, IL; White, IL; Gibson, IN; Knox, IN; Posey, IN, issued at 11:59 AM EDT, effective until 6 May 2026, 12:00 PM EDT. Residents are urged to avoid flooded roads and not to drive around barricades.

Flood Warning for Daviess, IN; Gibson, IN; Knox, IN; Pike, IN, issued at 11:58 AM EDT, effective until 11 May 2026, 2:00 PM EDT. Similar precautions apply regarding flooded areas.

Flood Warning for Gallatin, IL; Wabash, IL; White, IL; Gibson, IN; Posey, IN, issued at 10:11 AM CDT, effective until 8 May 2026, 1:00 AM CDT. Motorists should remain vigilant and avoid travel in flooded conditions.

The NWS emphasizes the importance of safety during these weather events. Turn around, don't drown when encountering flooded roads as most flood-related fatalities occur in vehicles. Be especially cautious at night when visibility is reduced.

For more detailed information and updates, residents can visit the NWS website at www.weather.gov.

Additional Reporting By: Jessica Storm; National Weather Service; NOAA; Indiana Department of Transportation

What this means

Residents in affected areas should prepare for potentially hazardous weather conditions. It is essential to stay updated with the latest alerts and take necessary precautions to ensure safety during flooding and strong winds.
