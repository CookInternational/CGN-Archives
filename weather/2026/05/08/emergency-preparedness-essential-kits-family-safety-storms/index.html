Emergency Preparedness: Essential Kits for Family Safety During Storms

A guide to assembling emergency kits to ensure safety during severe weather events.

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published At: 8 May 2026 at 3:33 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 9 May 2026 at 4:59 PM EDT

Emergency preparedness is crucial for families facing severe weather events. As storms can strike unexpectedly, having an emergency kit ready can make a significant difference in ensuring safety and survival.

Emergency kits should be tailored to the specific needs of your family and the types of severe weather you might encounter. Here are some essential items to include:

1. Water and Food: Store at least one gallon of water per person per day for at least three days, along with non-perishable food items that can sustain your family for a similar duration.

2. First Aid Supplies: A well-stocked first aid kit is essential. Include bandages, antiseptics, pain relievers, and any necessary prescription medications.

3. Flashlights and Batteries: Power outages are common during storms. Ensure you have flashlights and extra batteries to provide light when needed.

4. Whistle: A whistle can be a lifesaver in emergencies, helping to signal for help if you become trapped.

5. Multi-tool or Swiss Army Knife: This can be useful for a variety of tasks, from opening cans to making repairs.

6. Personal Hygiene Items: Include items like hand sanitizer, moist towelettes, and toilet paper to maintain hygiene during emergencies.

7. Important Documents: Keep copies of important documents such as insurance policies, identification, and medical information in a waterproof container.

8. Clothing and Blankets: Pack extra clothing and blankets to stay warm and dry, especially if you lose power.

9. Battery-Powered or Hand-Crank Radio: Stay informed about weather updates and emergency instructions with a reliable radio.

10. Cash: In case of power outages, ATMs may not be operational, so having some cash on hand is advisable.

Regularly check and update your emergency kit to ensure that food and medications are not expired. Additionally, involve all family members in the preparation process to ensure everyone knows where the kit is stored and how to use its contents.

By taking the time to prepare an emergency kit, families can enhance their safety and readiness for any severe weather that may arise. Stay informed about local weather conditions and follow any official guidance during storms.

What this means
