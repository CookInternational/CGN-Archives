Severe Weather Alert for 9 May 2026: Flood Warnings Issued Across Multiple Counties in Indiana & Illinois

Residents are urged to take precautions as severe flooding is expected in affected areas.

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published At: 9 May 2026 at 7:34 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 9 May 2026 at 7:34 PM EDT

Severe flooding is anticipated in several counties across Indiana and Illinois as the National Weather Service (NWS) has issued multiple flood warnings effective from 9 May 2026. The warnings highlight the urgency of the situation, with residents advised to take immediate precautions.

The flood warnings were issued for the following areas:

  • Crawford, IL; Lawrence, IL; Knox, IN; Sullivan, IN - Effective until 10 May 2026 at 11:00 PM EDT.
  • Edwards, IL; Gallatin, IL; Wabash, IL; White, IL; Gibson, IN; Knox, IN; Posey, IN - Effective until 11 May 2026 at 8:00 AM EDT.
  • Daviess, IN; Greene, IN; Knox, IN - Effective until 12 May 2026 at 2:51 AM EDT.
  • Lawrence, IN; Martin, IN - Effective until 12 May 2026 at 2:00 PM EDT.
  • Daviess, IN; Gibson, IN; Knox, IN; Pike, IN - Effective until 13 May 2026 at 4:34 AM EDT.
  • Daviess, IN; Gibson, IN; Knox, IN; Pike, IN - Effective until 15 May 2026 at 8:00 PM EDT.
  • Gibson, IN; Knox, IN - Effective until 16 May 2026 at 6:48 PM EDT.
  • Johnson, IN - Effective until 9 May 2026 at 11:00 PM EDT.

Residents in the affected areas are urged to heed the warnings and take necessary precautions. The NWS advises, "Turn around, don't drown when encountering flooded roads. Most flood deaths occur in vehicles." It is especially important to be cautious at night when it is harder to recognize the dangers of flooding.

Additional statements from the NWS are expected to be issued as the situation develops, with the next updates anticipated late tonight.

Additional Reporting By: National Weather Service; National Weather Service; National Weather Service; National Weather Service; National Weather Service

What this means

Residents in the affected counties should prepare for potential flooding by avoiding travel unless absolutely necessary. It is crucial to stay informed through local news and the National Weather Service for updates on the situation. If you encounter flooded roads, remember to turn around and seek alternative routes to ensure your safety.
