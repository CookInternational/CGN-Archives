Severe Weather Alert for 10 May 2026: Flood Warnings Issued for Southern Indiana Counties

Residents in Daviess, Greene, Knox, Gibson, Pike, Lawrence, Martin, and Jackson counties are urged to exercise caution.

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published 10 May 2026 at 6:06 PM EDT

Multiple flood warnings have been issued by the National Weather Service (NWS) for several counties in southern Indiana, including Daviess, Greene, Knox, Gibson, Pike, Lawrence, Martin, and Jackson. The warnings were issued on 10 May 2026 and are effective until various dates in mid-May, with the most urgent warnings set to expire as late as 16 May 2026.

The NWS has classified these warnings as severe and has emphasized the immediate urgency for residents to take precautions. The primary instruction is clear: "Turn around, don't drown when encountering flooded roads. Most flood deaths occur in vehicles."

Residents are particularly cautioned to be vigilant at night when it is harder to recognize the dangers of flooding. The NWS advises that caution is also urged when walking near riverbanks.

Additional information regarding the flood warnings and safety measures can be found on the NWS website. The next statements regarding these warnings are expected to be issued late tonight.

Additional Reporting By: National Weather Service; National Weather Service; National Weather Service; National Weather Service; National Weather Service

What this means

Residents in the affected counties should remain alert and prepared for potential flooding. It is crucial to avoid driving through flooded areas and to stay informed about the latest updates from the National Weather Service. Being cautious during nighttime is especially important as visibility is reduced.
