Lightning Safety for Schools, Sports, and Outdoor Events

Essential guidelines to protect students and attendees during thunderstorms

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published At: 7 May 2026 at 2:32 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 9 May 2026 at 4:59 PM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | With the arrival of spring, thunderstorms are becoming increasingly common, raising concerns about lightning safety for schools, sports teams, and outdoor events. The National Weather Service emphasizes the importance of preparedness and awareness to minimize risks associated with lightning strikes.

Lightning can strike as far as 10 miles away from a thunderstorm, making it crucial for schools and event organizers to have a clear plan in place. The first step is to monitor weather conditions closely, especially during outdoor activities.

When thunder is heard or lightning is seen, it is essential to take immediate action. The safest place to be during a lightning storm is indoors. Schools should have protocols to move students quickly to safe locations, such as gymnasiums or classrooms, away from windows and doors.

For outdoor events, organizers should establish a lightning safety plan that includes monitoring weather updates and having a designated shelter area. Attendees should be informed about the plan and the importance of seeking shelter at the first sign of a storm.

In addition to seeking shelter, it is important to avoid using electrical appliances and plumbing during a lightning storm, as lightning can cause power surges and damage. Schools and event organizers should also educate students and attendees about the dangers of lightning and the importance of following safety protocols.

As part of their safety measures, schools should conduct regular drills to ensure that students and staff are familiar with the lightning safety plan. This preparation can help reduce panic and confusion during an actual storm.

Outdoor sports teams should also have a clear communication strategy to alert players and spectators of impending storms. Coaches and officials should be trained to recognize the signs of severe weather and to act swiftly to ensure everyone's safety.

In summary, lightning safety is a critical concern for schools and outdoor events as thunderstorms become more prevalent. By implementing effective safety measures and educating students and attendees, the risks associated with lightning can be significantly reduced.
