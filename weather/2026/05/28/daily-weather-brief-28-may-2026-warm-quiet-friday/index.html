Daily Weather Brief for 28 May 2026: Warm, Quiet Conditions Set Up a Pleasant Friday

Indianapolis stays warm and mostly quiet Thursday before lower humidity and pleasant weather carry into Friday and Saturday.

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published At: 28 May 2026 at 6:43 PM EDT · Last Updated At: 28 May 2026 at 6:43 PM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | Indianapolis is moving into a quieter weather pattern after earlier fog and flood concerns, with warm temperatures Thursday and more comfortable conditions expected to carry into Friday.

The current Indianapolis forecast shows mostly sunny conditions around 83°F, with Thursday’s high near 84°F and a low near 54°F. Friday is expected to be partly sunny with low humidity and a high near 81°F. Saturday looks pleasant, with intervals of clouds and sun and a high near 78°F.

That means the main weather story for central Indiana is not severe storms. It is a welcome stretch of warmer, calmer weather after unsettled days. Evening plans should be in good shape for most of the Indianapolis area, with cooler temperatures overnight and a comfortable start Friday morning.

Drivers should still watch for any lingering low-lying moisture near rivers, creeks or areas that recently saw flooding. But for most readers, the practical forecast is straightforward: mild evening, cooler overnight and a good Friday for commuting, errands, school events and outdoor plans.

Looking ahead, the weekend starts on a pleasant note. Saturday’s high in the upper 70s should feel comfortable for outdoor events, parks, errands and local sports. Humidity should be lower than earlier in the week, making the air feel less heavy.

CGN News is not publishing a Severe Weather Alert with this row because the current general forecast does not support new severe-storm language for Indianapolis. If the National Weather Service issues a new official alert, CGN News will update weather coverage accordingly.
