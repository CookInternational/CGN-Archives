Severe Weather Alert for 28 May 2026: Dense Fog Advisory and River Flood Warnings Continue Across Parts of Indiana

Dense fog and river flood warnings are the main official weather concerns for parts of Indiana this morning.

By Jessica Storm · Weather · Published At: 28 May 2026 at 8:13 AM EDT · Last Updated At: 28 May 2026 at 8:13 AM EDT

INDIANAPOLIS | A Dense Fog Advisory and river Flood Warnings are the central weather concerns for parts of Indiana this morning, while Indianapolis begins the day with fog before conditions improve.

The National Weather Service Indianapolis office listed active watches, warnings and advisories this morning, including dense fog and flood-related products. Drivers should use low-beam headlights, slow down and leave extra following distance where visibility is reduced.

Flood warnings remain a separate hazard from the morning fog. River flooding can persist even after rain ends, and water over roads, near riverbanks or around low-lying areas can remain dangerous. Motorists should not drive through flooded roads or around barricades.

This alert should not be read as a tornado or severe-thunderstorm warning. The supported public-safety language this morning is fog, reduced visibility, river flooding and general hazard awareness.

Additional Reporting By: National Weather Service Indianapolis; NOAA

What this means

For readers, the morning risk is practical: drive slowly in fog, use headlights, avoid flooded roads and keep checking official weather updates before traveling near low-lying or river-adjacent areas.
